The Mimililu & Co. Jewelry E-commerce Site: Project Overview

First draft, not finalized – Work In Progress

The Mimililu & Co. Jewelry E-commerce Site (“E-commerce site”) is a fictional online jewelry retailer.

It is open to the public: anonymous users have limited access to the system, and users can make purchases if they become members.

The site aims to be attractive, simple and usable.
Any user with a web browser can access the site, browse or search for jewelry that are in stock, and view the details.

The details of jewelry include the:

  • name,
  • marketing description,
  • technical descriptions: materials & finish, size, fittings, category, the collection.

Anonymous users can add selected jewelry to a shopping cart.
Users can also be members, and the membership application process collects details about the customer in the same way as at most online sites.
To purchase jeweleries, users must log in using their membership details.
If a user has just joined as a member, he is logged in automatically.
After selecting jeweleries for purchase, the user can place an order. An order is shipped immediately and a confirmation sent by email.

Behind the scenes, the system also allows the stock managers to add new shipments of jewelry to the database.
The website manage can also add new jewelry products, collections, materials & finishes, size categories, etc…

Functional and systems requirements:

  • The online Mimililu & Co. site is primarily aimed as an e-commerce site to sell jewelry.
  • The system doesn’t manage accounting, stock control, payroll, ordering, and other tasks.
  • Users may select jewelry and add them to a shopping basket. Users may purchase the items in their shopping baskets for up to one day after the first item is added to the basket. Users have only one shopping basket each and may empty their basket at any time.
  • Users of the site may be anonymous and can remain anonymous until they agree to purchase the items in the shopping basket.
  • To purchase items in a shopping basket, the user must log in to the system. To log in, a user must have an account. To get an account, a user must provide at least his surname, first name, one address line, a city, a zip code, a country, his birth date, an email address, and a password. The email address is used as the user’s login name. The user may also optionally provide a middle initial, a title, two additional address lines, a state, a telephone number, and a fax number.
  • When a user purchases jewelry, his order is archived.
  • A user may receive a percentage discount on the price of an order. A discount can be levied on a particular day, a minimum threshold quantity, or given to a regular customer.
  • An order may have a delivery charge that is levied according to the user’s location and the delivery mode. Delivery modes include sea mail, regular mail, and express mail. An order may also have a note that is directed to the delivery company; for example, a note might indicate to “leave the products inside the mailbox.”
  • Jewelry are classified into broad categories of necklaces/pendants, rings, earrings, bracelets, charms. Jewelry also have a name, a marketing description, and a technical descriptions; marketing descriptions are optional free-form text that are typically a review of the jewelry; technical descriptions are required fields such as materials/finish, size, fittings, category, and collection.
  • Jeweleries are made from different materials and finishes, including silver, gold, etc…
  • A shopping basket is an incomplete order that contains items. It can be converted to a completed order after the user logs in. Each item in an order is for a particular jewelry, a quantity of that jewelry to be purchased, and a price per item. The price of the jewelry is always the price of the first item of jewelry added to the shopping cart, which in turn is always the cheapest available inventory price.
  • The quantities of jeweleries in the shopping basket can be updated by the user, and items can be removed from the shopping basket.
  • The jeweleries available for sale are stored in an inventory. Each inventory record has a date added and is for a particular item. The inventory contains a stock quantity available at a particular per-item price. There can be several inventory records for a jewelry, representing different shipments that arrived at the e-commerce site on different dates or that have a different price.
  • The user will always be advertised prices from the cheapest inventory for each jewelry. When a user adds a jewelry to her shopping basket, she is guaranteed this price.
  • A user can purchase only jeweleries that are in stock.
  • When a user converts his shopping basket to an order, the availability of sufficient inventory to complete the order is checked. If insufficient jewelry is available, the user is alerted, and the quantities in the shopping basket are updated; this situation can occur if a user adds more jewelry to his basket than is available.
  • When sufficient inventory is available to complete an order, the quantity of jewelry in the inventory is reduced as the order is finalized. The inventory reduced is always the oldest inventory of that jewelry.
